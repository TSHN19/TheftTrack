About the App

This app uses advanced machine learning models to predict theft crime in Chicago.


Neighborhood Component Feature Selection (NCFS)
NCFS is a method that selects the most important factors affecting crime, like population and poverty rates. It filters out less useful information, helping the model focus on what matters most for accurate predictions.


Gradient Boosting Decision Tree (GBDT)
GBDT takes the selected factors from NCFS and builds a series of decision trees to predict theft crime. This application improves accuracy by adding methods to handle complex data and make better forecasts.

About Us

Theft Track is a crime forecasting application focused on providing clear and concise predictions and user-friendly information about future theft crimes in Chicago. According to the Chicago Data Portal records, theft is one of the leading crime type in Chicago. This application aims to help citizens and visitors by offering the latest and future information on theft crimes, spreading awareness, and helping allocate resources efficiently within the area.

Predictions are generated using various factors, including Population Density, Poverty Rate, Education Rate, Inflation Rate, Unemployment Rate, Consumer Price Index, and Gross Domestic Product, which influence the forecast.
